Thinking a lot about the end these days.

A preliminary assignment for the MA in Visual Communication programme at Aalto University.

Document size: A6 (105x148mm). Inkjet print on offset paper (80gsm). 16 pages, staple-bound, saddle stitched booklet, with die-cut rounded corners. Type set in San Francisco Pro Text, Noto Nastaliq & Jameel Nastaleeq. Copyright © 2021 Saad Khurshid. All rights reserved.

Intro:

This booklet aims to visualise how a new culture takes over and eradicates the preexisting culture. It focuses on the coexistence of the two common languages of Pakistan, English and Urdu.

Format:

The booklet takes advantage of the opposite reading directions of English (left-to-right) and Urdu (right-to-left), to create a palindrome-like reading format.

If read from the left-hand side, the book starts in English, then the text gradually transitions into Urdu. And vice versa, from the right-hand side, the book starts in Urdu and ends in English.

Content:

For the content of the book I wrote down some thoughts I had during the pandemic, after witnessing the loss of several people. Consistent with the theme of contrasting cultures: in English I write about the experience of death in an objective and scientific way. Whereas, in Urdu, the text focuses on the life after death. It uses the Urdu language to convey fantastical, spiritual experiences of an afterlife.
